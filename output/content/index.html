Connaught Place Rajiv Chowk

CP, also known as Connaught Place, is a grand white arcade located in New Delhi that acts as a central hub for shopping, dining, offices, nightlife, metro access, heritage tours, and the lively atmosphere of daily life in the city.

Why Connaught Place matters

CP is more than just a market - it is the bustling heart of Lutyens' Delhi, blending heritage charm with modern conveniences. As a metro interchange, financial hub, dining hotspot, and popular meeting place, the iconic white Georgian colonnades encase one of India's most renowned urban districts. Underground and nearby markets seamlessly connect upscale boutiques with budget-friendly shopping choices.

Heritage

Colonial design, Indian life

Originally built to serve as the new imperial capital, CP now attracts a diverse crowd including office workers, students, shoppers, tourists, street vendors, and late-night diners.

Connectivity

Metro-first destination

The connection at Rajiv Chowk station between the Blue and Yellow Lines provides easy access to CP, one of the most convenient areas in Delhi.

History: from imperial commercial plaza to Delhi's everyday meeting point

The establishment of New Delhi as the capital by the British led to the creation of Connaught Place, transforming the area from villages and sacred sites into a bustling business center exuding discipline, harmony, and imperial grandeur.

The selection of Delhi as the new capital of British India led to the development of Lutyens' Delhi.

1920sPlanning evolves

Architects W. H. Nicholls and Robert Tor Russell collaborated on the layout design of the commercial plaza.

1929-1933Construction

The design of Connaught Place includes circular layouts, covered walkways, retail stores, corporate spaces, and historic movie theaters.

1947 onwardIndianization

CP evolves from a space for the colonial elite to a vibrant public area with Indian restaurants, bookstores, and cultural institutions.

1995-presentRajiv Chowk era

Official name changes happen, Metro is introduced, brands expand worldwide, and conservation talks continue.

Architecture and layout

Connaught Place is famous for its white colonnades, Georgian-neoclassical style, and circular layout, inspired by Bath's Royal Crescent. The design was adapted for Delhi to form a bustling commercial hub with covered arcades, high ceilings, and spacious verandahs designed to endure the hot and rainy climate.

Navigation tip: The Inner Circle comprises blocks A-F surrounding Central Park, while the Outer Circle consists of blocks G-P forming the outer ring. Janpath, Parliament Street, Baba Kharak Singh Marg, Barakhamba Road, and other radial roads connect Central Park to the rest of New Delhi.

A commercial powerhouse

Connaught Place is renowned as one of India's top business districts, thanks to its iconic status, prime location, and convenient transport connections. Its historic core remains low-rise to protect its legacy, while neighboring streets such as Barakhamba Road and Kasturba Gandhi Marg are flanked by impressive high-rise office buildings.

This creates CP's distinctive commercial environment: upscale offices and brands above, busy commuter traffic below, budget-friendly shopping beneath, and towering corporate structures on the outskirts.

Current 2026 context: restoration, cleanliness and future mobility

Connaught Place requires ongoing maintenance as a living heritage district, with efforts such as repainting facades, cleaning corridors, repairing footpaths, and improving Central Park/subway infrastructure. The discussion about making CP completely vehicle-free continues, with traders worried about business access and planners pushing for a more pedestrian-friendly atmosphere.

Facade revival

NDMC has planned painting and repair projects for the facades of both the Inner and Outer Circles, with continued public tenders and restoration work in progress after previous event-driven enhancements.

Sanitation drive

NDMC has implemented block-level monitoring to improve cleanliness in CP by enhancing regular sweeping, garbage disposal, and accountability.

Pedestrian future

The concept of a car-free or low-traffic CP idea has been a topic of discussion for some time, and its success may hinge on enhancements to parking, signage, and last-mile shuttles.

Visitor guide: how to plan your CP trip

How to reach

Best option: Use the Blue and Yellow Lines of the Delhi Metro to get to Rajiv Chowk station, with Janpath, Barakhamba Road, or Patel Chowk stations as possible alternatives depending on your final destination.

Best time

It is best to take a walk from October to March, with early mornings and evenings as the preferred times in the summer. Weekday afternoons are less crowded, while Friday and Saturday evenings are more popular.

Typical hours

Most shops usually open from 10:30 AM to 11:00 AM and close around 8:30 PM to 10:00 PM, whereas restaurants, cafes, and bars tend to stay open later. Business hours

Safety and comfort

Ride the metro to steer clear of parking problems, keep your belongings safe in crowded areas, opt for comfortable footwear, and don't forget your Metro exit number at Rajiv Chowk.
